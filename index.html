✦ ARG Glossary — hover to learn ✦

TINAG "This Is Not A Game." The defining principle of ARGs — the fiction is presented as real, and players are expected to treat it as such. Rabbit Hole The entry point or chain of clues that pulls a player deeper into an ARG's story. Going down the rabbit hole means committing to the solve. Puppetmaster The creator(s) controlling an ARG from behind the curtain. They author puzzles, play characters, and guide the story in response to player actions. Trailhead The initial clue or page that brings someone into an ARG. Usually designed to look like an accidental discovery. Collective Detective The player community working together to solve an ARG. No one person cracks it — the community functions as one distributed mind. Curtain The line separating players from Puppetmasters. Lifting the curtain means revealing the game's true creators — which breaks the TINAG illusion. Lurker Someone who follows an ARG and its community without actively participating in solves. The ratio of lurkers to active solvers is typically 5:1 to 20:1. Analog Horror A horror subgenre using retro media aesthetics — VHS tapes, old TV broadcasts, corrupted files. Often overlaps with ARGs. Think Local 58 or Gemini Home Entertainment. Rubbernecker A step above a lurker — someone who follows the community and may post, but avoids direct contact with in-game characters or registering through in-game websites. Steganography Hiding secret messages inside ordinary-looking files — images, audio, video. A core ARG tool. The message is invisible unless you know to look. Staple When an ARG abruptly ends without resolution, leaving solvers with no payoff for their time. One of the most dreaded outcomes in the community. Walkthrough A linear narrative of how an ARG was discovered and solved, step by step. Useful for newcomers catching up on concluded games. Beast The first mainstream ARG, created for the film A.I. in 2001. It established almost every convention the genre still uses today — trailheads, Puppetmasters, the TINAG principle. Metatransparency The open secret that an ARG is a game even while maintaining the TINAG fiction. Players know it's a game but agree to play as if it isn't.
